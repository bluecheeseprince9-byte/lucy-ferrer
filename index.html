The Process

From confused
to closing day.
Here is the path.

No mystery. No steps you find out about too late. Just the clear, honest picture of what buying your first home in Wichita actually looks like when you have the right person walking with you.

1

Know Your Real Number Before You Look at a Single Property

The biggest mistake first-time buyers make is starting with the homes instead of starting with the numbers. You fall in love, then you find out the math does not work, and you spend the next three weeks trying to un-fall-in-love with a kitchen you cannot stop thinking about. Start with what you can genuinely afford. Then find the home that fits inside that picture. The playbook includes a simple affordability breakdown that tells you your real number in about ten minutes.

The move: Get a pre-approval letter from a lender before you tour anything. Not pre-qualified. Pre-approved. The difference matters because sellers in Wichita's 27-day market take pre-approved buyers seriously and pre-qualified buyers as possibilities.
2

Choose the Neighbourhood Before You Choose the House

A house can be renovated. A neighbourhood cannot. The kitchen can be updated. The commute cannot be shortened. The school district cannot be switched after you close. Most first-time buyers pick the home first and settle for the neighbourhood. The ones who are happiest five years later picked the neighbourhood first and found the best available home within it.

The move: Before your first showing, spend one weekend visiting your top two or three Wichita neighbourhoods. Walk them. Eat there. Drive the route to work or school. Let your gut weigh in before your eyes do.
3

Make an Offer That Means Something in This Market

Wichita homes are selling in about 27 days on average. That is not a market where you can think about it for two weeks. When the right property comes up, you need to be ready to move with confidence. That confidence comes from knowing your budget, knowing the neighbourhood, and having an agent who knows exactly how to structure an offer that gets taken seriously.

The move: When you find a property you love, ask Lucy to pull the last six months of closed sales on that specific street. Not the zip code average. The street. That tells you whether your offer is fair, competitive, or leaving money on the table.
4

Close With Clarity and Walk Away With Keys That Actually Feel Like Yours

The closing table is where first-time buyers often feel the most overwhelmed. Pages of documents, numbers that look bigger than expected, and a room full of people who all seem to know what is happening except you. With the right preparation and the right agent beside you, closing day stops being a source of anxiety and starts being exactly what it should be the day you become a homeowner.

The move: Read the playbook before your closing day appointment. Knowing what each document is for and what each number represents means you can sign with confidence instead of signing with fingers crossed.
